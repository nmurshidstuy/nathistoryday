Greensboro Sit-ins


  • Background
  • What Led Up To It
  • What Happened During It
  • The Result

The greensboro Sit ins left many people inspired to do the same. The next day, there were more than 20 African American men protesters who joined the protest. They were denied service by the staff and were harassed, but the protesters stood their ground. The following day, about 60 more people showed up and so on.

Finally, the store decides to serve the African Americans on July 25, 1960 and began a period of desegregation at the store. On top of that, a new group was formed called the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC). This group played major roles in the civil rights movement of the time. Thus, the impact of the Greensboro Sit Ins was fairly great.
